LAWS OF MOTION 02

Dictation of things.

Isaac Newton's Laws

English physicist and mathematician, who was the culminating figure of the Scientific Revolution of the 17th century. In optics, his discovery of the c omposition of white light integrated the phenomena of colours into the science of light. In mechanics, his three laws of motion resulted in the formulation of the law of universal gravitation. In mathematics, he was the original discoverer of the infinitesimal calculus.

They explain why, how and to what extent things change in motion, they detail the conditions for change that we intuitively recognise.

It’s all about how things interact via forces. It’s all about balance and proportions. It’s all deterministic (don’t bring up quantum mechanics).
Law of Inertia - The Condition For Acceleration
  A thing at rest will remain at rest and a thing with velocity will remain at that velocity unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.
Any change in velocity has a cause.
  Things keep on doing what they’re doing.
There are no spontaneous or random changes in motion.

For a thing with unchanging velocity, the forces (if present) are balanced - it's not accelerating - it is inertial.

The Force Equation Law - The Extent of Acceleration.
  Acceleration equals net (unbalanced) force divided by mass.
A change in velocity is due to a change in net force.
  The acceleration of a thing is proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.
Doubling the net force or halving the mass will double the acceleration.

Things don’t slow down because of an absence of force, they slow down because of the presence of force. Without friction things would roll forever.

Law of Action and reaction - The Symmetry of Acceleration
  For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
A thing can’t affect without being affected.
  All forces occur in opposing pairs.
To push off something is also to be pushed.

Things push against each other symmetrically such that force transfers.

RELATIVITY OF MOTION 03

Comparing things.

The general population ubiquitously describes motion relative to the Earth. “Relative to the Earth” is an implied addendum to every car speedometer and every other sentence of a fitness motivator. As a consequence it’s easy to think that the earth is an absolute reference. In actuality you can describe the motion of anything in relation to anything else in the universe.
The pilot flying that plane overhead is stationary relative to the plane.
The Earth is moving under your feet when you walk.
That turtle is hurtling at 107,826.05 km/hr relative to the sun.
The Sun is going around the Earth.
The (uniform) motion of any thing can only be determined in relation to another thing. Could you still say that the Earth still be moving if no other planets or stars existed?
There's relative motion between everything, and anything can be considered stationary.
Motion is entirely relative.

MOTION WITHIN FRAMES 04

Comparing Points of View.

When we’re thinking about the positions of things, it’s always in reference to some fixed coordinate space. For day to day life the Earth is the go to fixed point. It’s not always though, like, for example, when we think about how the Earth orbits the Sun.
This coordinate space is called our frame of reference.
Think of it as the center of the universe which everything moves around.
We can in fact choose anything to be the fix point for our frame of reference.
Galileo Galilei's Invariance

Italian natural philosopher, astronomer, and mathematician who made fundamental contributions to the sciences of motion, astronomy, and strength of materials and to the development of the scientific method. His formulation of (circular) inertia, the law of falling bodies, and parabolic trajectories marked the beginning of a fundamental change in the study of motion.

The Galilean Invariance - The laws of motion are the same in all inertial reference frames.

Since an inertial reference frame only has motion when compared to another reference frame.

It’s important to highlight that this invariance only holds for inertial reference frames. In accelerating reference frames there is an external force being applied which messes things up. Imagine being on a ship in perilous waters and contrast that to a smooth sail on still waters.

MOTION'S SPEED LIMIT 05

The absolute exception.

LIGHT

The speed of light is constant.
In a vacuum, it slows down when moving through a medium.
The speed of light is not relative, it’s absolute.
Any beam of light anywhere will be measured as moving at 299,792,458 m/s from Every. Single. Reference frame.
That's about 7.5 times around the Earth every second.
